City transit officials confirmed Tuesday that the Harbor Line extension will open on March 3, four months behind schedule. The delay stems from a signaling contractor bankruptcy discovered during a routine audit in September. The extension adds six stations and is projected to carry 41,000 riders daily. Fares will hold at $2.75 through the first year, after which the board will review pricing. Two of the six stations still lack elevator access; officials say retrofits will finish by summer.
