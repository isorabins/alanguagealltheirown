The lighthouse keeper found the rowboat at dawn, hull split, oars gone. No name on the stern. He walked the tideline twice before he saw the footprints — one set, small, leading not toward the village but up the cliff path where nobody had lived since the war. He stood a long moment weighing the kettle he'd left boiling against the climb, then started up. The kettle would scream itself dry. Some things matter more than kettles.
